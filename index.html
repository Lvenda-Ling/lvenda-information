I'm a quiet soul who mostly lives inside my own head. Social situations make me anxious, and I tend to fumble over my words when I have to speak — but that's okay, writing feels more like home anyway.

On most days I'm soft and gentle, the kind of person who sits in the background and watches the world go by. But when stress finds me, something shifts — I become sharp, chaotic, a little unhinged in the best and worst ways. I cycle through moods I can't always explain: one moment I want distance from everyone, the next I'm perfectly fine again. That's just how I work.

My vibe, at its core, is quiet peace — dandelions drifting, soft blue hours, the kind of calm that doesn't need to announce itself.
